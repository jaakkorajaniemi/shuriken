{% trans 'Case examples - Direct startup growth advisory' %}

  • Operational support and Japan entry PMO for an European startup with over €50 million in total investment.
  • Mentor for a Japanese consumer app startup in a government-sponsored startup acceleration program.
  • Provided pitch training for a Japanese manufacturing startup planning to enter the Singaporean market.

The challenge of startup founders is that they have very limited time for other things than sales and growth. Maneuvering through the valley of death, transforming a loose group of colleagues into an organization, and entering a new market which operates on different market dynamics are next to impossible to master alone. As a mentor, I can advise on best practices and fill-in a broad variety of temporary bilingual operational roles, combining state-of-the-art techniques such as agile project management, lean startup and general business administration frameworks.

{% trans 'Case examples - Corporate co-innovation advisory' %}

  • Performed business needs analysis for a corporation and listed 100 startups as collaboration candidates.
  • Conducted qualitative due diligence on a multi-million venture capital investment in real estate industry.
  • Provided hands-on negotiation support for co-innovation between a Japanese corporation and a global startup.

Large corporations often have plenty of assets, but no permission to spend resources on exploring disruptive new ideas, no resources to cope with shortening innovation lifecycle, too slow processes to allow rapid agile development, and little experience of intercultural teams and global market entry. As a consultant, I assess the needs and goals of the corporation and provide a framework for startup collaboration which reduces the likelihood of failure and gives an idea of the best practices to proceed. I also facilitate ground-level meetings in both English and Japanese.

{% trans 'Case examples - Public sector startup advisory' %}

  • Invited over 190 global startups to Japan and matched them with over 200 Japanese large corporations.
  • Conducted foreign startup ecosystem research in the Nordic countries and Russia for a local ministry.
  • Collaborated with local foreign embassies and the metropolitan government to showcase global startups.

The government wants to increase local ecosystem competitiveness, attract skilled global talent, foster new innovation, and support the society as a whole while under constant pressure and observation from taxpayers. Furthermore, the government is constantly developing policies and innovation schemes that work in the local context. As an advisor, I can support the government both in a policy researcher role and also as an organizer of specific government innovation and startup initiatives.

My mission is to make the Japanese innovation ecosystem more global and competitive. Currently, most startups and corporations struggle with the information barrier caused by gaps in cultural and linguistic understanding. As a resident of Japan, it is in my utmost interest to help realize a better society to deliver a happier life to all of us.
